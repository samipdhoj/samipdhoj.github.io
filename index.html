Tour: Everest Base Camp

The Everest Base Camp Trek, also known as the EBC Trek, is an all-inclusive 14-day trekking package from Kathmandu, Nepal, to the base camp of the highest mountain in the world. The walking tour is high on the bucket list of things to do for adventurers around the globe. It’s because the hike to Everest Base Camp allows you to see a magnificent close-up view of towering Mount Everest, the highest peak in the world, from every angle.

Tour: Dhaulagiri Base Camp

The Mt. Dhaulagiri (8167m) trek is not for beginners. Ascending through the French Pass (5360m) will be a test of your endurance, but with well-prepared acclimatization beforehand will make it easier for you. Follow trails through Hidden Valley set among impressive white snow-capped mountains and ruggedly desolate landscapes. The Hidden Valley is surrounded on one side by the Boudha Himal and Himal Chuli in the west, Ganesh Himal in the south and Sringi Himal in the North. This area has not changed in centuries due to it being cut off from the southern lowlands by deep gorges and rivers and from Tibet in the north by snow-covered passes.

Tour: Kanchenjunga Base Camp

The Kanchenjunga Base Camp Trek provides the most varied trekking experience of the major 8,000 m peak trekking in Nepal. Straddling the border with Sikkim, Kanchenjunga at 8,586m is the worlds third highest mountain, while its base camp trekking also known as KBC Trekking is only for off the beaten adventurous trekkers. This world third highest mountain has a tremendous mass with 5 major peaks ridge. This 5 ridge creates different five icy mass glaciers radiating away from the main ridge of Kanchenjunga.

Tour: Mardi Himal Base Camp

Mardi Himal Base Camp is a newly explored and hidden gem of the Annapurna region, located in the north-central part of Nepal. This is a spectacular as well as an easy trip on the Annapurna route. The visitors will get a more peculiar experience than other Himalayan trips and it is also a great wilderness experience. The Mardi Himal Base Camp Trekking offers much attractive flora and fauna, unique culture, undisturbed wildlife, old houses, waterfalls, dense forests, and incredible Himalayan vistas including Mount Machhapuchhare (Fishtail), Hiunchuli, and a range of Mount Dhaulagiri and Annapurna.
